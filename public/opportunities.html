Opportunities


Hazard Engineering Summer Internships - Deadline: February 15th, 2017

The Natural Hazards Engineering Research Infrastructure – Research Experience for Undergraduates (NHERI-REU) is now accepting applications for its summer research program.

The eight-week paid internships at NHERI's experimental sites give undergrads an amazing chance to practice hands-on hazard engineering and start building their professional network.

Applications are online: https://www.designsafe-ci.org/learning-center/reu/

For questions about #REU or more information, contact them via email: reu-request@designsafe-ci.org.

City of San Diego & ACEC Scholarship - Deadline: February 27th, 2017

The City of San Diego and ACEC are looking to give a $2,000 scholarship to a student in each of the following field: Engineering (Civil, Geotechnical, Structural, Electrical, Environmental, Construction and Mechanical), and Land Surveying! Check out the easy application information here!

ASCE Transportation Scholarship - Deadline: March 3rd, 2017

ASCE Transportation Group, a sub-group of ASCE, is offering a $1,000 scholarship opportunity for students interested in transportation. For more information, click here!

Concrete Masonry Association of California and Nevada Scholarship - Deadline: March 20th, 2017

CMACN, a non profit organization in association with concrete masonry block manufacturers, has established a scholarship program in order to promote the study of concrete masonry as a subject. Recipients will receive $500 and a copy of their latest edition of “Design of Reinforced Masonry Structures.”

For more information, including how to apply, click here.

ACI San Diego Scholarship Opportunity - Deadline: April 24, 2017

The San Diego Chapter of American Concrete Institute (ACI) has approved one or more scholarships to be granted to local students in the coming academic year. Candidates are selected by the ACI SD Scholarship Committee from an extremely competitive pool of applicants. All candidates must meet the following criteria:

  1. Full time student (minimum 12 units/credit hours) enrolled in a University in the San Diego area for the Fall 2017 or Spring 2018 academic school year
  2. Must be pursuing a bachelors or graduate degree in a concrete related field (i.e. Engineering, Architecture, Chemistry, Construction Management, etc.)
  3. Demonstrated academic strength, leadership ability, and industry involvement

For more information, or to submit an application, go to: http://aci-sandiego.org/form.php?form_id=22

Next Step Ministries Summer Internships

In 2007, Next Step Ministries started an internship program for construction and engineering majors that wanted to use their skills out in the mission field, designing and building homes for communities in need. They currently partner with about 20 CM/Engineering programs across the country and provide summer co-ops in 16 communities here in the US, Haiti, and Guatemala. They have multiple open positions as Construction Managers and Construction Team Members for the 2017 summer.
For more information, you can read this flyer or visit their website page.

The San Diego Section of the American Society of Civil Engineers Younger Member Forum (ASCE YMF) is collecting applications for the purpose of awarding student scholarships to those interested in pursuing a degree in civil engineering or a related field at a four-year university. They will be two, $1,000 scholarships awarded to UCSD undergraduate or first year graduate students.

Ongoing Opportunities 

SCSE Sidekicks - Mentorship Program

The purpose of SCSE Sidekicks is to ease the transition of underclassmen and new transfers into the SE program at UCSD, and to encourage increased involvement in SCSE from all students. This is a good way for Sidekicks (upperclassmen) to give back, and for the Heroes (underclassmen and new transfers) to get advice on classes and get more involved in SCSE! Registration for the 2016-2017 year has closed.  

SCSE Incentive Program

The SCSE incentive program was created in order to encourage members to take advantage of the many opportunities we offer and to become more involved with the organization. Students who accumulate certain amounts of points are awarded with prizes like T-shirts and reimbursement for the Pacific Southwest Conference, as described in the SCSE Incentive Program 2016-2017 PDF.
Contact Gabby Morales at gmorales133@gmail.com to find out how many points you have.
